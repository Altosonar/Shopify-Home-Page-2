Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our pattern making services

How long does pattern development typically take?

Pattern development timeline varies based on complexity and requirements. On average:

  • Simple patterns: 2-3 business days
  • Medium complexity: 5-7 business days
  • Complex designs or multiple sizes: 7-14 business days

We provide a more accurate timeline during our initial consultation based on your specific project needs.

What file formats do you work with?

We work with all major pattern and CAD file formats including:

  • Input formats: Gerber (.pat), DXF, AutoCAD (.dwg), Adobe Illustrator (.ai), PDF, JPEG, PNG, and physical patterns
  • Output formats: Gerber Accumark (.pat, .hdr, .con), DXF, AutoCAD (.dwg), PDF, HPGL, and other industry-standard formats
  • Grading files: Full size sets with nested markers for optimal fabric utilization

If you have specific format requirements, we can accommodate most industry standards.

Do you provide physical pattern printing?

Yes, we offer comprehensive physical pattern printing services:

  • Large-format plotter printing on pattern paper or card stock
  • True-to-scale patterns with clear markings and notations
  • Nest markers for fabric optimization
  • International shipping of physical patterns
  • Option for digital files only or both digital and physical copies

Our plotters accommodate paper widths up to 72 inches, suitable for even the largest garment patterns.

Can you work from sketches or photos?

Absolutely! We regularly work from various source materials including:

  • Hand-drawn sketches and illustrations
  • Photographs of existing garments
  • Technical flats and fashion illustrations
  • Physical samples or existing patterns
  • 3D designs and virtual samples

For sketches and photos, we'll discuss measurements, fit preferences, and fabric characteristics to ensure the pattern matches your vision accurately.
